# Canon_R50V_SmallRig_Cage_Grip_Improvement
This 3d-printable grip replaces the tiny grip from the SmallRig R50V cage with a bigger, more ergonomic grip.
![Assembled grip](https://github.com/AnselmSeeholzer/Canon_R50V_Smallrig_Cage_Grip_Improvement/blob/main/pictures_renders/1.jpg?raw=true)
![Render1](https://github.com/AnselmSeeholzer/Canon_R50V_Smallrig_Cage_Grip_Improvement/blob/main/pictures_renders/Render1.png?raw=true) ![Render2](https://github.com/AnselmSeeholzer/Canon_R50V_Smallrig_Cage_Grip_Improvement/blob/main/pictures_renders/Render2.png?raw=true)

TPU is recommended as it is a soft material.
The 3d-printable grip fits snug onto the old cage and is secured by two M3 DIN 912 50 mm screws with two M3 DIN 934 nuts as well as two 1/4 inch 10 mm screws.
![Assembled grip](https://github.com/AnselmSeeholzer/Canon_R50V_Smallrig_Cage_Grip_Improvement/blob/main/pictures_renders/9.jpg?raw=true)

Supports are needed in one section. To guarantee a tight fit on the SmallRig cage, I used the sharpie-support-trick.
The grip is printed standing normally.
![Supports](https://github.com/AnselmSeeholzer/Canon_R50V_Smallrig_Cage_Grip_Improvement/blob/main/pictures_renders/8.jpg?raw=true)
